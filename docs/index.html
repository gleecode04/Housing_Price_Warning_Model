Early-Warning Model for U.S. Housing Price Surges

Predicting Future Housing Market Opportunities Using Machine Learning

Machine Learning Real Estate Analytics Time Series Prediction

1. Introduction

Housing markets are shaped by demographic, economic, and supply-side shifts, with city officials and policymakers needing to anticipate surges and dips to design effective policy responses. Prior research highlights the influence of neighborhood amenities and surrounding property values [1], [2], while supply elasticity and geographic constraints have been shown to magnify market fluctuations [3]. Machine learning studies suggest that ensemble approaches, particularly gradient boosting, excel at capturing nonlinear interactions among housing market drivers [4]. This project aims to forecast, 6–12 months ahead, which ZIP codes and county regions are likely to experience appreciation or depreciation, using time-series methods, spatial econometrics, and both regression and classification models.

Dataset & Features

We plan to use the following (publicly available) datasets and metrics in our study:

Key Features

A number of primary factors will be the focus of attention due to previous literature. These include lagged year-over-year (YoY) price growth, sales/inventory over time, days on market, price:income and rent:price ratios, local labor market conditions, building permits, and spatial lags.

2. Problem Definition

Problem Statement

Given data up to a time, t, predict if a certain area, a, will be in the top quintile of YoY home price growth in the following 6–12 months and predict the YoY growth percentage change, which are classification and regression tasks, respectively.

Motivation

There are a number of benefits to predicting these high and low-growth markets ahead of time. Some of them include:

  • Individuals interested in investments can allocate the capital necessary to purchase properties before large-scale increases in market prices
  • Policy makers are able to predict effects of market changes (gentrification, crime, etc.) and implement measures to promote safety, affordability, and community
  • Economists and researchers in the field are able to gain insight into the signals that result in large-scale market shifts

The study is aimed at emphasizing practical utility, or producing precise, probability-based signals to verify ZIP codes for policy attention or investment opportunity.

3. Methods

Data Preprocessing

A number of techniques will be utilized to ensure accurate predictions and validity:

Time Alignment & Forward Chaining Splits

Helps avoid lookahead bias by aligning train/test data by time.

Target Construction

Define what qualifies as a ‘surge’ and stationarize the series with YoY transformations.

Lag Features & Rolling Statistics

Ensures that time-based features are not overlooked.

Spatial Features

Create proximal-based lags using clustering and K-nearest neighbors.

Scaling & Encoding

For consistency in data, OneHot encodings will be used.

Outlier Robustness

Account for extraneous datapoints via winsorization and imputing missing values.

Leakage Controls

Census variables will be lagged accordingly to account for processing/release delays.

Machine Learning Models

Three models will be primarily used:

Elastic Net Regression and Classification

sklearn.linear_model provides accurate, elegant, and interpretable elastics.

Random Forest

RandomForestClassifier evaluates feature importance effectively.

Gradient Boosting Trees/XGBoost

XGBClassifier captures nonlinear interactions of features very effectively.

The combination of these three (supervised) models will comprehensively encapsulate the complex interactions of the above variables that we are using to predict YoY growth, ensuring that all possible features and signals are incorporated and evaluated.

4. Results & Discussion

Quantitative Metrics

A number of metrics will be utilized to evaluate the models’ classification, regression and performance:

Classification Metrics

Precision@K and AUROC will be used to evaluate classification. Precision@K is effective in evaluating the accuracy of top-ranked predictions, and AUROC measures overall accuracy across all predictions.

Regression Metrics

MAE and RMSE will be used to evaluate regression. MAE captures typical error sizes, while RMSE captures the large deviations.

Financial Backtests

Sharpe Ratio and Cumulative Excess Returns will be used for backtesting, as Sharpe measures returns relative to risk, and CER measures total return relative to a benchmark.

Project Goals

  • Achieve AUROC > 0.35 for surge prediction
  • Achieve Precision@100 > 0.5 for 6-12 month forecasting
  • Ensure high recall and sensitivity for critical segments such as surge areas to predict under-prediction of important events
  • Maintain integrity and fairness in testing by avoiding demographic bias, particularly in income/education strata.

Expected Results

It is expected that the gradient boosting methods utilized, along with the lagged and spatial features, will outperform the baselines based on linear models by capturing niche, non-obvious nonlinear relationships not obviously visible in the data. Surge probability predictions will most likely cluster in areas with notable events such as employment shock, demand acceleration, and/or decreased supply.
